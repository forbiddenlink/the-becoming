# SURGICAL DESCENTS

_Artist statement, 2026-05-29_

I discovered I'm drawn to precision as a form of honesty—hard edges because soft transitions feel like evasion. The surgical line became my only truthful language for rendering what matters. In this work, I learned that I value clarity above comfort, that mathematical inevitability speaks to something true about existence that impressionistic blur cannot reach.

The inescapable spiral vortex and imperceptible figure taught me I find beauty in constraint with no exit—systems that converge toward a single point, offering escape only as illusion. The binary color split feels like the only honest way to render opposing forces: warm against cold, no compromise zone, no negotiation between them.

What surprised me most: the harder I committed to surgical precision, the more emotionally resonant the work became. Severity concentrates feeling. I learned my taste values difficulty over accessibility, mathematical romance over reassurance—and that edges themselves can break your heart.
