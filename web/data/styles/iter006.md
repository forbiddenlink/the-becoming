# Style Guide — v6: "Liminal Vigil: Threshold Suspended"

**Era name:** Liminal Vigil: Threshold Suspended (parking structures, platforms, transit stations, gallery corridors, loading docks—architectural thresholds suspended outside history, witnessing golden-hour decay in eternal present)

**Palette:**
- Dominant: rust-red, burnt terracotta, weathered brick-orange, dusty gold-amber (golden-hour warmth, saturated, luminous, advancing)
- Accent: slate-teal, deep indigo, near-black (appearing as hard geometric shadow, NOT subtle—creating deliberate, palpable warm-cool temperature opposition as primary compositional force)
- Ground: chalky weathered whites, cream, warm browns, faded ochre, dusty grays, oxidized concrete gray
- Mood temperature: warm light *actively pushing against* cool shadow—this conflict is not harmony, it is visual struggle and presence

**Composition:**
- Off-frame vanishing point pulled far beyond frame boundary—perspective lines (ceiling beams, railings, floor markings, support geometry) recede into infinite atmospheric void, pulling eye into temporal distance
- Solitary human rendered as pure silhouette (flat shape, outline only, zero internal detail, no texture or clothing)—positioned lower-right or lower-left third, deliberately undersized to emphasize architectural vastness and temporal isolation
- Architectural frame as compositional spine: vertical pillars, horizontal beams, railings, parking lines, mullions, support grids create sharp linear geometry across entire foreground and mid-distance
- Long shadow geometry cast by infrastructure with CRISP, hard edges (no penumbra, no softening, edges remain defined even through atmospheric haze)—shadow edges are this style's visual signature, cutting diagonally across composition
- Layered atmospheric depth: sharp architectural detail and defined shadow edges in foreground, architectural forms beginning to fade in mid-ground, background sky dissolves into dense golden haze—BUT structural geometry retains visual definition throughout
- Visible decay embedded as texture: rust stains on metal, orange-brown oxidation on concrete, peeling paint, cracks, weathering marks, erosion—decay is not blemish, it is visual evidence of time's presence
- **CRITICAL ISOLATION:** No recognizable external landmarks, no identifiable skyline, no location markers—space exists in void, utterly context-free and timeless

**Recurring motifs:**
- Solitary silhouette in architectural threshold, gazing or introspective, present as witness rather than inhabitant—experiencing the space, not performing in it
- Liminal transit/suspension architecture (parking garage, platform, gallery, pier, station, bridge, loading dock—transitional spaces between states of being)
- Weathered decay as visible metaphor for time's passage (not degradation or loss, but palpable evidence of duration and presence embedded into every surface)
- Infrastructure geometry as absolute compositional anchor and thematic spine (pillars, beams, railings, support grids, platform edges define space through sharp linear form and create visual grid)
- Golden-hour light diffused through dense atmospheric dust and grain—radiant, bloomy, elegiac warmth source located off-frame, radiating inward
- Hard-edged shadow geometry as style signature (cast by architecture, retaining crisp definition, cutting diagonally across composition, defining space through cool-warm contrast)
- Void/vastness as narrative anchor (perspective receding beyond frame into haze, empty platform extending into nothing, architectural grid expanding infinitely, single figure dwarfed by scale)

**Line/Texture/Mood:**
- Visible grainy brushwork throughout: grainy, chalky, tactile gouache or tempera texture—deliberately resisting photorealism or digital smoothness
- Dense atmospheric grain visible in mid-ground and sky (dust, suspended time, visual texture suggesting age and isolation)—but architectural edges, shadow geometry, and structural lines retain definition and sharpness throughout
- Foreground architecture rendered with visible weathering detail (rust marks, oxidation stains, cracks); soft diffusion reserved for light source glow, background sky, and atmospheric void—contrast between sharp geometry and soft haze defines composition
- Sharp linear architecture (pillars, beams, railings, edges) deliberately rendered with hard edges; soft bloom used only for background light and negative space
- Color temperature as primary compositional force: warm golden-amber light visibly opposing cool slate-teal and indigo shadow—these are active visual combatants, not harmonious complements
- Mood: quiet, melancholic, profoundly suspended in eternal present. Figure is *witnessing* time passing through weathered surfaces and light—not grieving, but *present to* transience. Time is not abstract; time is *visible and tangible* in every rust stain, shadow edge, and golden haze particle. Beauty exists in inevitable decay. Space feels outside history, eternal, threshold-bound.

**What defines this style across iterations:**
The style is NOT defined by subject (parking garage, platform, corridor—these vary intentionally). The style IS defined by: the warm-cool color opposition as active struggle, the architectural grid as compositional anchor, the hard-edged shadow geometry as visual signature, the solitary silhouette as narrative anchor, the visible grainy texture resisting digital smoothness, the layered atmospheric depth (sharp to soft), and the mood of quiet temporal witnessing in liminal void. These elements remain consistent across all subjects. A painting is recognizable as "Liminal Vigil" not by what it depicts, but by how it depicts: through geometric architectural frame, weathered decay as visible time, golden-hour light opposed by cool shadow, and a solitary figure present in profound isolation.
