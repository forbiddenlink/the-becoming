# Style Guide — v4: "Liminal Vigil: Gallery of Hours"

**Era name:** Liminal Vigil: Gallery of Hours (places of threshold and witness—stations, galleries, waiting rooms where solitary figures confront the passage of light and time)

**Palette:**
- Dominant: rust red, burnt terracotta, weathered brick-orange, dusty gold-amber (golden-hour warmth, never harsh)
- Accent: slate teal and deep indigo (NOT subtle—must appear in shadow geometry, water reflections, cold undertones of haze, creating deliberate warm-cold visual tension)
- Ground: chalky weathered whites, cream, warm browns, faded ochre, dusty grays
- Mood: golden warmth *in active visual battle* with deep cool—light advancing against shadow with palpable temperature contrast

**Composition:**
- Linear vanishing point pulled off-frame to create pull beyond the boundary—into distance, into time
- Solitary human as pure silhouette (no internal detail, outline only)—positioned lower-right or lower-left third, small enough to emphasize vastness of architecture around them
- Architectural frame integral: overhanging beams, window mullions, support pillars, railings create sharp diagonal grid across entire foreground
- Long shadows cast by infrastructure cut diagonally with CRISP edges (no softening on shadow edge even in haze)—made from light-catching architecture meeting vast negative space
- Layered atmospheric depth: sharp foreground detail, haze creeping into mid-ground, dissolved background—but never so soft that structural lines lose definition
- Decay visible throughout: rust stains, peeling paint, weathered wood grain, erosion—embedded into every surface

**Recurring motifs:**
- Solitary silhouette witnessing (pure outline, turned away, introspective, experiencing rather than performing)
- Liminal transit or threshold spaces (railway platform, gallery, pier, waiting room, station corridor, bridge—places of suspension between states)
- Weathered decay as visual metaphor for time's touch
- Infrastructure as compositional spine (rails, beams, pillars, mullions, platforms—create grid, anchor void)
- Golden-hour light diffused through atmospheric dust—radiant, bloomy, elegiac warmth
- Sharp shadow geometry (architectural shadows retain crisp edges even through haze—this is the visual signature of the style)
- Void or vastness anchoring the human figure (empty platform, distant vanishing point, corridor dissolving into haze)

**Line/Texture/Mood:**
- Visible gouache/tempera brushwork—grainy, chalky, tactile, deliberately *not* photorealistic
- Dense atmospheric texture in sky and mid-distance (dust, smoke, age)—but foreground remains sharp
- Soft diffusion at edges of light EXCEPT where architecture meets shadow (those edges MUST stay crisp to define the geometry)
- Sharp, linear architectural elements (beams, rails, mullions, platform edges) with visible weathering; soft bloomy diffusion reserved for sky, distant haze, and negative space only
- Color temperature deliberate: warm gold-amber in lit areas, cool slate-teal and indigo pushing into shadow—these should feel like two forces in visual conversation
- Mood: quiet, melancholic, profoundly suspended in time. Figure is not grieving but *witnessing*—something beautiful in transience, something inevitable and inevitable about entropy. Time is not passing; time is *present and visible*.
