# Style Guide — v3: "Liminal Vigil"

**Era name:** Liminal Vigil (spaces suspended in time—stations, platforms, thresholds where waiting crystallizes into eternity)

**Palette:** 
- Dominant: rust red, burnt terracotta, weathered brick orange, dusty gold-amber (golden-hour warmth)
- Accent: deep teal and slate blue (shadows, water, structural undertones—cold counterpoint)
- Ground: chalky grays, warm weathered browns, faded cream, dusty ochre
- Mood: golden warmth pressed against deep cold—nostalgia fighting entropy

**Composition:**
- Strong linear vanishing point (tracks, rails, platform edge, bridge support) drawn slightly off-frame to pull eye beyond boundary
- Solitary human as pure silhouette (no modeling or detail)—small, positioned lower-right or lower-left third, bearing witness
- Architectural frame integral to composition: overhanging structure, support beams, pillars create rhythmic diagonal shadow geometry across entire foreground
- Long geometric shadows (cast by beams, roof, infrastructure) cut diagonally left-to-right or top-to-bottom, making time visually tangible
- Deep atmospheric haze obscures background, creating layered depth (foreground sharp, middle-distance soft, distance dissolved)

**Recurring motifs:**
- Solitary human silhouette (pure outline, small, introspective, witnessing)
- Liminal transit spaces (railway platform, pier, bridge, waiting room—threshold between states)
- Decay and weathering (rust stains, peeling paint, cracked wood, erosion) as visual metaphor for transience
- Infrastructure as spine (pillars, beams, rails, rails create compositional grid anchoring the void)
- Golden-hour light diffused through haze—radiant bloom without direct sun, glow that feels both warm and elegiac
- Void or vastness (empty platform, distant vanishing point, horizon lost in haze)

**Line/Texture/Mood:**
- Gouache and tempera visible brushwork—grainy, chalky texture, not photorealistic
- Dense atmospheric texture in sky and middle-distance (almost dust or cigarette smoke quality)
- Soft bloomy bloom at edges of light except where architecture meets shadow (sharp contrast preserved)
- Sharp architectural lines (beams, rails, platform edge) remain crisp; soft diffusion reserved for sky, haze, and negative space
- Subtle color temperature shifts: warm gold-amber in lit areas, cool slate-teal creeping into shadow
- Mood: quiet, melancholic, haunted by time's passage, sense of suspended waiting—figure is not sad but witnessing something inevitable and beautiful in its transience
