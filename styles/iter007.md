# Style Guide — v7: "Liminal Vigil: Threshold Suspended — Geometric Isolation"

**Era name:** Liminal Vigil: Threshold Suspended — Geometric Isolation (brutalist interiors, parking structures, transit platforms, gallery corridors, loading docks—architectural thresholds where time pools, vanishing point pulled infinitely sideways and upward, perspective never resolving into "exit," figure perpetually trapped in geometric suspension)

**Palette:**
- Dominant: rust-red, burnt terracotta, weathered brick-orange, dusty gold-amber (golden-hour warmth, saturated, luminous, advancing, *physically eating into* cool shadow)
- Accent: slate-teal, deep indigo, near-black (hard geometric shadow, cutting diagonally, NOT subtle—warm light and cool dark are active visual combatants)
- Ground: chalky weathered whites, cream, warm browns, faded ochre, dusty grays, oxidized concrete gray, rust-stain orange
- Mood temperature: warm gold-amber light *actively pushing against* cool shadow—visual struggle as primary compositional force, not harmony

**Composition:**
- Vanishing point pulled infinitely sideways and upward *beyond visible frame*—perspective lines (pillars, beams, ceiling geometry, support grid) recede with NO clear terminus; if a vanishing point appears, obscure it or shift it laterally, making space feel labyrinthine rather than destination-bound
- Architecture extends left-right and up-down beyond frame edge—viewer feels trapped *within* the grid, not approaching it; lateral extension as critical as depth
- Solitary human reduced to pure silhouette (flat outline, ZERO internal detail)—positioned deliberately *off-center* in lower-left or lower-right third, NOT centered; undersized to emphasize isolation; figure gazing inward or downward (introspective, not directed toward horizon)
- Architectural frame as compositional spine: vertical pillars, horizontal beams, ceiling grid, support structure create rigid geometry across entire composition; this geometric grid is *inescapable* and defines space entirely through linear form
- Long shadow geometry cast by infrastructure with CRISP hard edges (no penumbra, no softening)—shadow edges retain definition even through atmospheric haze; shadows cut diagonally across foreground and mid-ground, defining space through cool-warm temperature opposition
- Layered atmospheric depth: sharp architectural detail and hard shadow edges in foreground, architectural forms fading into diffused golden haze in mid-ground and background; structural geometry remains *visually defined* throughout (ribs of pillars, beam edges visible even in haze), BUT *texture* softens (grain becomes denser, form becomes more ethereal)
- Visible weathering embedded as tactile texture: rust streaks following gravity and structural seams (not random), orange-brown oxidation, peeling paint, cracks, water stains, erosion—decay is *motion-evidence* (gravity, time, water), not static blemish
- **CRITICAL ISOLATION:** No recognizable landmarks, no external skyline, no location markers—space exists in architectural void, utterly context-free, timeless, and *inescapable*

**Recurring motifs:**
- Solitary silhouette positioned off-center, introspective, present as *permanent* witness trapped in geometric threshold (not traveler passing through)
- Brutalist/industrial architecture (parking garage, loading dock, civic center, platform, warehouse, support structure, brutalist concrete interior)—architectural grid extends beyond visible frame laterally and vertically, suggesting infinite repetition and inescapability
- Weathering as motion-evidence: rust follows gravity, water stains mark seepage paths, oxidation stains cluster at structural seams—decay is *physical history* embedded into surface, visible proof that time has *moved through* the space
- Infrastructure geometry as absolute compositional anchor: pillars, beams, ceiling grid, support ribs, structural lines create rigid geometry that *traps* perspective; vanishing point either pulled infinitely beyond frame or shifted laterally (never resolving into clear exit)
- Golden-hour light diffused through dense atmospheric dust and grain—radiant, bloomy, elegiac warmth source located off-frame, radiating inward and *opposing* cool structural shadow
- Hard-edged shadow geometry as style signature: cast by infrastructure, retaining crisp definition, cutting diagonally, creating visual grid that *locks* figure in place
- Void/vastness as narrative anchor: perspective extending infinitely without terminus, architectural grid repeating beyond visible boundary, single figure dwarfed and *trapped* by scale (not inspired by it)

**Line/Texture/Mood:**
- Visible grainy brushwork throughout: chalky, tactile gouache or tempera texture deliberately resisting photorealism or digital smoothness—every surface has *weight* and *physicality*
- Dense atmospheric grain visible in mid-ground and sky: dust suggesting suspended time, visual texture of age—but architectural edges, shadow geometry, and structural lines retain *crisp definition* throughout (ribs of concrete pillars visible even in haze)
- Foreground architecture rendered with visible weathering detail (rust tracks, oxidation stains, water marks, cracks); soft diffusion reserved for light source glow, background sky, and atmospheric void—contrast between sharp geometry and soft haze defines spatial recession
- Sharp linear architecture (pillars, beams, edges) rendered with hard edges deliberately; soft bloom used only for background light and atmospheric haze—never softening structural geometry itself
- Color temperature as primary compositional force: warm golden-amber light *visibly opposing* cool slate-teal and indigo shadow—these are active visual combatants, palpable as physical forces
- Mood: quiet, melancholic, profoundly suspended in eternal present. Figure is *trapped* and *witnessing* simultaneously. Time is visible and tangible in every rust streak that follows gravity, every water stain, every oxidation mark—time has *moved*, but the figure *remains*. Space is *inescapable*; beauty exists in the inevitability of decay and eternal suspension. The figure is neither grieving nor celebrating—simply *present* in a moment that never ends.

**What defines this style across iterations:**
The style is NOT defined by subject. It IS defined by: (1) architectural grid extending *beyond* visible frame laterally/vertically (space feels inescapable, not destination-bound), (2) warm-cool color opposition as active visual struggle, (3) hard-edged shadow geometry as signature element, (4) off-center solitary silhouette as permanent witness (not traveler), (5) weathering as motion-evidence (rust, water, oxidation following physical laws), (6) visible grainy gouache texture, (7) layered atmospheric depth with architectural definition retained throughout, (8) mood of quiet eternal suspension and inescapability. A painting is recognizable as "Liminal Vigil: Geometric Isolation" by how it *traps* the viewer within its architectural frame, maintains crisp structural definition even in haze, opposes warm and cool as palpable forces, and positions the figure as *permanently suspended* witness to visible, tactile time.
