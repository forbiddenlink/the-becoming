# Style Guide — v2: "Lighthouse Vigil"

**Era name:** Lighthouse Vigil (era of coastal solitude, liminal waiting spaces, fading sentinels)

**Palette:** 
- Dominant: rust red, burnt terracotta, weathered brick orange, dusty gold-amber
- Accent: deep teal and slate blue (shadows, water, undertones)
- Ground: chalky grays, warm weathered browns, faded cream
- Mood: golden-hour warmth meeting cold shadow—nostalgia against resignation

**Composition:**
- Strong vertical or diagonal framing—tower, bridge, roofline as spine guiding eye downward to figure
- Solitary human silhouette placed strictly rule-of-thirds off-center (lower third, frame edge)
- Architectural element frames figure without dominating—archway, tower edge, platform rail
- Long, thick geometric shadows cast across foreground—time made physically visible
- Vanishing point slightly off-frame—drawing eye beyond the boundary, suggesting continuation

**Recurring motifs:**
- Solitary human silhouette (small within landscape, introspective, bearing witness)
- Decay and weathering (rust, cracks, patina, erosion) as metaphor for time and transience
- Coastal or liminal transit stations (lighthouse, pier, platform, bridge—spaces of watching and waiting)
- Golden-hour light cutting through dense haze—glow that feels both warm and melancholic
- Water or vast emptiness (void, horizon, suspended time)

**Line/Texture/Mood:**
- Gouache and tempera-inspired brushwork—visible paint texture, not photorealistic rendering
- Dense grainy/chalky texture especially in sky, haze, and weathered surfaces
- Thick atmospheric bloom softening edges except for sharp architectural lines (contrast key)
- Diffused, glowing quality—light scattered through haze rather than directly shining
- Mood: quiet, melancholic, contemplative, haunted by time; sense of bearing witness to something passing away
