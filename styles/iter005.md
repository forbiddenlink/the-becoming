# Style Guide — v5: "Liminal Vigil: Threshold Suspended"

**Era name:** Liminal Vigil: Threshold Suspended (stations, galleries, parking structures, waiting rooms existing in visual and temporal isolation—spaces of pure threshold severed from external context)

**Palette:**
- Dominant: rust red, burnt terracotta, weathered brick-orange, dusty gold-amber (golden-hour warmth, saturated but never harsh)
- Accent: slate teal, deep indigo, near-black (must appear as hard shadow geometry, NOT subtle—creating deliberate warm-cool temperature battle)
- Ground: chalky weathered whites, cream, warm browns, faded ochre, dusty grays, oxidized concrete gray
- Mood: golden warmth *in active visual conflict* with deep cool shadow—light advancing against void, palpable temperature opposition

**Composition:**
- Off-frame vanishing point pulled far beyond boundary—perspective lines recede into infinite visual void, pulling eye into distance and time
- Solitary human as pure silhouette (outline only, no internal detail, never filled with color or pattern)—positioned lower-right or lower-left third, deliberately small to emphasize vastness of space
- Architectural frame as absolute spine: pillars, beams, railings, support geometry create sharp linear grid across entire foreground and mid-distance
- Long shadows cast by infrastructure cut diagonally with CRISP, hard edges (no softening on shadow edge, even through atmospheric haze)—shadow geometry is the style's visual signature
- Layered atmospheric depth: sharp architectural detail in foreground, dense haze creeping into mid-ground, dissolved background—BUT structural lines and shadow edges retain definition throughout
- Visible decay: rust stains, oxidation, peeling paint, weathered concrete, erosion marks—embedded into every surface, especially architectural elements
- **CRITICAL ISOLATION:** No recognizable external references, no identifiable skylines or location markers—the liminal space exists in visual void, context-free, eternal

**Recurring motifs:**
- Solitary silhouette witnessing from architectural threshold (turned away or introspective, present but not performing, experiencing the space rather than inhabiting it)
- Liminal transit/suspension spaces (parking garage, railway platform, gallery corridor, pier, station, bridge—places between states)
- Weathered decay as visual metaphor for time's visible touch (not degradation, but evidence of presence and passage)
- Infrastructure as compositional and thematic anchor (pillars, beams, railings, platforms, mullions—these create geometric grid and define the void)
- Golden-hour light diffused through dense atmospheric dust—radiant, bloomy, elegiac warmth radiating from specific light source
- Sharp shadow geometry as style signature (architectural shadows retain crisp edges, cut diagonally, define space through contrast)
- Void/vastness as narrative anchor (empty platform extending into haze, perspective lines converging beyond frame, no human activity except solitary figure)

**Line/Texture/Mood:**
- Visible brushwork: grainy, chalky, tactile gouache or tempera—deliberately NOT photorealistic or digital-smooth
- Dense atmospheric grain in mid-distance and sky (dust, age, suspended time)—but foreground architecture remains sharp and defined
- Soft diffusion only at background edges and sky; hard edges reserved for architectural elements and shadow geometry—this contrast defines the composition
- Sharp linear architecture (beams, pillars, railings, platform edges) rendered with visible weathering detail; soft bloomy diffusion used only for light source, sky, and negative space
- Color temperature as active compositional force: warm gold-amber light pushing against cool slate-teal and indigo shadow as visual opponents, not complements
- Mood: quiet, melancholic, profoundly suspended. Figure is *witnessing* time passing, not grieving—beauty in transience and inevitable decay. Time is not abstract; time is *visible and present* in every weathered surface and cast shadow. The space feels eternal, outside history.
