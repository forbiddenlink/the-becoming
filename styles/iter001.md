# Style Guide — v1: "Last Light"

**Era name:** Last Light (era of liminal spaces, autumnal atmosphere, quiet waiting)

**Palette:** 
- Dominant: rust red, terracotta, burnt orange, dusty gold
- Accent: teal, deep slate blue, cold shadow tones
- Ground: weathered grays, warm browns, faded pastels
- Mood: golden-hour nostalgia with melancholic cool undertones

**Composition:**
- Strong linear perspective (tracks, rooflines, platform edges) drawing eye to center or vanishing point
- Silhouetted human figure off-center (rule of thirds), grounding the scene emotionally
- Architectural framing (buildings, arches, canopies) creating "tunnel" effect
- Long, geometric shadows stretching across foreground—time made visible

**Recurring motifs:**
- Solitary human silhouette (everyman figure, introspection, waiting)
- Decay and weathering (cracks, rust, patina, wear) as metaphor for time and transience
- Trains, platforms, liminal transit spaces (journey, change, suspension)
- Golden-hour light cutting through haze and shadow

**Line/Texture/Mood:**
- Brushed, painted quality mimicking gouache or tempera (not photorealistic)
- Grainy, chalky digital texture visible especially in sky and weathered surfaces
- Strong contrasts between sharp architectural lines and soft, diffused light
- Thick atmosphere—bloom and haze softening edges, creating dreamlike quality
- Mood: quiet, introspective, nostalgic, contemplative; sense of time stopped but always passing
